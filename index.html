Project Explanation

This video presents a dynamic visualization of data collected by NASA's Terra satellite. It demonstrates how remote sensing technologies capture Earth's surface changes, including vegetation, urban expansion, and atmospheric phenomena. The visualization highlights key trends and anomalies, helping scientists and the public better understand our planet's evolving environment.

About Terra Satellite

  • Launched: December 18, 1999
  • Mission: Part of NASA’s Earth Observing System (EOS)
  • Instruments: MODIS, ASTER, CERES, MISR, MOPITT
  • Purpose: Monitors Earth's atmosphere, land, and oceans
  • Achievements: Provided critical climate data, mapped global fires, tracked pollution, and more
